About Me

Thinking About Time,
Power & Society

I am a philosopher working at the intersection of social and political theory and the analytical philosophy of time. My research asks how temporal experience, memory, and anticipation shape political institutions, collective identities, and conceptions of justice.

My current project investigates the temporality of democratic deliberation — how different time horizons, from electoral cycles to intergenerational obligations, structure the possibilities and limits of democratic self-governance.

I am also interested in the metaphysics of persistence and change, particularly as it bears on questions of personal and collective identity over time.
