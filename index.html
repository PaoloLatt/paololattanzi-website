About Me

Bridging Philosophy
and the World

I am a PhD student in Philosophy at Universität Siegen, Germany, with a strong foundation in analytic metaphysics and social theory. My research focuses on Philosophy of Time, Ontology, the Foundations of Physics, and Analytical Social Theory.

Alongside my doctoral work, I serve as an Honorary Professor at IU Internationale Hochschule in Hamburg, where I teach Web Analytics and Search Engine Marketing — bridging academic inquiry with real-world digital innovation.

Prior to returning to academia full-time, I built over a decade of experience leading performance marketing teams across Hamburg, working with clients and organisations such as Essity, DAAD, and Allianz.

Research Areas

Areas of Inquiry

My work spans five interconnected fields within analytic philosophy and social theory.

01

Philosophy of Time

Investigating the metaphysical dimensions of temporal experience — passage, the present moment, McTaggart's A- and B-series, and the deep relationship between time, change, and causation.

Temporal Ontology McTaggart A/B-Theory
02

Analytic Metaphysics

Engaging with core problems in analytic ontology — persistence through time, identity, modality, and the structure of reality as approached through the tools of analytic philosophy.

Ontology Persistence Modality
03

Analytical Social Theory

Applying analytic philosophical tools to questions of society, power, and political economy — including contributions to Marxist systems theory and international relations.

Marxist Theory IR Theory Political Economy
